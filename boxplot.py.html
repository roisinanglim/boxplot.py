      ### Boxplot  Inventor
      ------------------------------
      John Tukey created and introduced the boxplot graph in 1977 as part of his toolkit for exporying data analytics. Today boxplots are one of the most frequently used statistical graphics [1].  
      ​
      ### Traditional Boxplot
      -------------------------
      The boxplot is a way of displaying the distribution of data based on the five number summary, minimum, upper quartile,lower quartile,interquartile, median and maximum. 
      ​
      ![boxplot.peg.JPG](attachment:boxplot.peg.JPG)
      ​
      ​
      - The median 
      - two hinges, the upper and lower fourths (quartiles) 
      - the data values adjacent to the upper and lower fences, which lie 1.5 times the inter-fourth range from
      the median 
      - two whiskers that connect the hinges to the fences 
      - (potential) out-liers, individual points further away from the median than the extremes [1]

      Boxplot Inventor¶

      John Tukey created and introduced the boxplot graph in 1977 as part of his toolkit for exporying data analytics. Today boxplots are one of the most frequently used statistical graphics [1].

      Traditional Boxplot¶

      The boxplot is a way of displaying the distribution of data based on the five number summary, minimum, upper quartile,lower quartile,interquartile, median and maximum.

      • The median
      • two hinges, the upper and lower fourths (quartiles)
      • the data values adjacent to the upper and lower fences, which lie 1.5 times the inter-fourth range from the median
      • two whiskers that connect the hinges to the fences
      • (potential) out-liers, individual points further away from the median than the extremes [1]

      Variations of the boxplot¶

      Due to their practicallity overtime boxplots have evolved into a wealth of variations and enhancements.The variations have consisted of changes in both the grapical display and the definitions of the original graph. Examples of the variations include the violin,notched,beanplot and variable width graphs displayed and discussed below.[1]

      ## When can a boxplot be used? 
      -----------------------------------------------
      ​
      * A boxplot can be used to show the distribution of data
      * To help identify potential outliers. 
      * Visually compare groups whilst taking into account the distribution of your data. 
      * Used on a sample size of five or more [2]
      ​

      ### Interpreting the boxplot based on Titantic dataset 
      -------------------------------------------------------------
      ​
      The boxplot above compares the age of passangers that survived against those that died. 
      ​
      - The points above the whiskers identify potential outliers that need to be investigated.The outliers are larger on the unsurvived group. 
      - The median age of 29 is the same across both groups. 
      - Lower whisker of the data is aged between 1-20 years. 
      - Interquartile ranges between 21-39 years. 
      - Upper whisker is different for both groups.The survived group ranges from 39-62 and the unsurvived ranges from 39-65.

      ### Variations of the boxplot 
      ​
      The violinplot below displays the median as a dot and the interquartile range as a line rather than a box. 
      ​
      #### Interpreting the violinplot
      -------------------------------
      ​
      * The median and the interquartile range are the same for both groups 
      * There is a larger distribution of people that survived below 15 years of age compared to those that died.
      * There is a larger distribution of people that died between 20 - 35 when compared to those that survived 
      * A larger number 40-60 year olds survived when compared to those that died
